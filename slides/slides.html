what is money?

  • money is something used as a medium of exchange between equal values of work and resources.
  • more info

history of money

the most basic mediums of exchange are commodities (cattle, shells, nails, tobacco, cotton, copper, silver, gold...)

precious metals have been preferred because of their physical properties

  • scarcity
  • durability
  • divisibility
  • distinct look and sound
  • homogeneity
  • malleability
  • beauty

instead of carrying commodities, societies developed paper notes, token coins, credit cards, and electronic bank accounts to act as placeholders for actual money


instead of standardizing paper notes on direct commodity values, societies based paper notes on fiat declarations of value.


these fiat declarations of value allow central banks to create more placeholder money at will, currently at the rate of $40-85 billion USD per month (more info)

why Bitcoin?

  • the global currencies of today are political tools of nation-states controlled by central banks
    • these central banks are competing to devalue their currencies for the sake of boosting domestic corporations in global markets
    • more info
  • Bitcoin is an alternative designed to decentralize control of money

fiat vs crypto

wallet

fiat

  • physical fiat requires carrying around change
  • digital fiat requires trusting and paying 3rd party

crypto

  • your wallet is a file and you do with it as you please
  • full node wallets like bitcoin-qt
  • thin client wallets like electrum
  • online client-side wallets like blockchain.info
  • paper/brain/vanity wallets
  • soon hardware wallets

currency creation

fiat

  • central banks can print money, thus inflating currency supply and devaluaing existing currency, at will

crypto

  • currency is distributed at roughly the rate of 50 coins per 10 minutes or 7200 coins per day to those verifying the transactions.
  • rate halves every 4 years, so currently at 25 coins per 10 minutes or 3600 coins per day
  • currently 11.034 million bitcoins created
  • will never be more than 21 million bitcoins

transactions

fiat

  • slow, closed on weekends
  • 2-4% fees
  • reversible through chargebacks

crypto

  • instant for unconfirmed, ~10 minutes per confirmation
  • 24/7
  • < 1% fees
  • irreversible

privacy

fiat

  • payments are public to surveillance states
  • traced to your legal name

crypto

  • transactions are public to everyone on the blockchain
  • traced to pseudonyms
  • capable of true anonymity with tor and coin mixing services, or proposed zerocoin extension

exchanging between

fiat and crypto

  • local exchanges like localbitcoins
  • online exchanges like bitfloor, mtgox, etc
  • online sellers like coinbase, bitinstant, ziggap, btcquick, etc
  • local atms soon
  • historical price chart
